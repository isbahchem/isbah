Analytical Chemistry

Analytical Chemistry is the chemistry that deals mostly with the composition of substances.

Definitions

Chemical composition of natural and artificial materials deals in analytical chemistry and determining the chemical composition qualitatively and quantitatively.

According to David Harvey: "Modern Analytical Chemistry"

The sience of inventing and applying the concepts, principles and strategies for measuring the characteristics of chemical systems and species.

Qualitative Analysis
Identify chemical species that may be present in material or substance.
Quantitative Analysis
Amount or concentration of chemical specie in substance.

Analytes are the components of a sample that are to be determined.

Role of Analytical Chemistry

Analytical Chemistry is applied throughout industry, medicine and all the sciences. Consider a few examples, "The concentration of oxygen and of carbon are determined in millions of blood samples everyday and used to diagnose and treat illness."

Quantities of hydrocarbons, nitrogen oxides and caron monoxides present in automobiles exhaust gasses are measured to assess the effectiveness of smog control devices. Quantitaive measurement of ionized calcium in blood serum help diagnose parathyroid disease in humans.

Qunatitative analytical measurements also play a vital role in many research areas in Chemistry, Biology, Geology, Physics and other sciences. For example, quantitative measurements of pottasium, calcium and sodium ions in the body fluids of animals permits physiologists to study the role of these ions in nerve signal conduction as well as muscle contraction and relaxation.

(Skoogh Fundamental Analytical Chemistry 8ed 2004)
